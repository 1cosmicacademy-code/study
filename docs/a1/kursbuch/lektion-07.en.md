## 2. 🔢 Plurals in German (Der Plural)

In English, we usually add "-s" or "-es" (cat → cats, box → boxes). In German, there are different ways to make plurals. Let's learn the most common ones!

### The Basic Rule:

| Singular Article | Plural Article |
|:----------------:|:--------------:|
| **der** → | **die** |
| **die** → | **die** |
| **das** → | **die** |

> All nouns in plural take the article **die**! Easy, right?

### 🟢 Way 1: Add **-e** (most common for masculine words)

| Singular | Plural | Pronunciation | English |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **der Tisch** | **die Tische** | **dee TISH-uh** | tables |
| **der Fisch** | **die Fische** | **dee FISH-uh** | fishes |
| **der Salat** | **die Salate** | **dee zah-LAH-tuh** | salads |
| **der Kuchen** | **die Kuchen** | — (same word!) | cakes |
| **der Schinken** | **die Schinken** | — (same word!) | hams |

### 🟡 Way 2: Add **-n** or **-en** (mostly for feminine words)

| Singular | Plural | Pronunciation | English |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **die Banane** | **die Bananen** | **dee bah-NAH-nen** | bananas |
| **die Tomate** | **die Tomaten** | **dee toh-MAH-ten** | tomatoes |
| **die Orange** | **die Orangen** | **dee oh-RAN-jhen** | oranges |
| **die Kartoffel** | **die Kartoffeln** | **dee kar-TOF-eln** | potatoes |
| **die Gurke** | **die Gurken** | **dee GOOR-ken** | cucumbers |

### 🟠 Way 3: Add **-er** (mostly for neuter words + vowel change)

| Singular | Plural | Pronunciation | English |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **das Ei** | **die Eier** | **dee EYE-er** | eggs |
| **das Kind** | **die Kinder** | **dee KIN-der** | children |

### 🔵 Way 4: Add **-s** (for foreign words)

| Singular | Plural | Pronunciation | English |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **das Auto** | **die Autos** | **dee OW-tohs** | cars |
| **das Café** | **die Cafés** | **dee kah-FAYS** | cafés |

### 🟣 Way 5: Change the vowel (Umlaut only — no extra ending)

Some words just change the vowel (a → ä, o → ö, u → ü) and that's the plural!

| Singular | Plural | Pronunciation | English |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **der Apfel** | **die Äpfel** | **dee EP-fel** | apples |
| **der Vater** | **die Väter** | **dee FAY-ter** | fathers |
| **der Bruder** | **die Brüder** | **dee BRÜ-der** | brothers |
| **die Mutter** | **die Mütter** | **dee MÜ-ter** | mothers |

### 🟤 Way 6: Vowel change + add **-e**

| Singular | Plural | Pronunciation | English |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------|
| **der Sohn** | **die Söhne** | **dee ZÖH-nuh** | sons |
| **der Hof** | **die Höfe** | **dee HÖH-fuh** | courtyards |

> 🎯 **Summary for plurals:**
> - Feminine words mostly add **-n/-en** (die Banane → die Bananen)
> - Masculine words mostly add **-e** (der Tisch → die Tische)
> - Neuter words may add **-er** (das Ei → die Eier)
> - Some words don't change in the plural (der Kuchen → die Kuchen — same word!)
> - **Best tip:** Learn the plural form with every new word from the start!

## 3. 🚫 Negation in German (Negation)

In German there are two ways to say "no" or "not". One is for verbs and adjectives, the other is for nouns. Let's learn the difference!

### Way 1: **nicht** = "not" (for negating verbs or adjectives)

Use **nicht** when you want to say someone does NOT do something, or something is NOT something.

**📌 Word order:**
> **Verb ... nicht.** — verb + ... + nicht (often at the end)

#### Examples with verbs:

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| **Ich esse **nicht**.** | I **don't** eat. |
| **Ich trinke **nicht**.** | I **don't** drink. |
| **Er arbeitet **nicht**.** | He **doesn't** work. |
| **Sie schläft **nicht**.** | She **doesn't** sleep. |
| **Das ist **nicht** gut.** | That is **not** good. |
| **Der Kaffee ist **nicht** heiß.** | The coffee is **not** hot. |

> **nicht** negates the verb or adjective. Put it at the **end of the sentence** most of the time!

### Way 2: **kein / keine** = "no / not a / not any" (for negating nouns)

Use **kein** when you want to say there is NO something (I don't have... / there isn't...). It changes depending on the gender of the noun:

| Noun type | Word | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Masculine (der) | **kein** | **kein** Kaffee — no coffee |
| Feminine (die) | **keine** | **keine** Milch — no milk |
| Neuter (das) | **kein** | **kein** Wasser — no water |
| Plural (die) | **keine** | **keine** Äpfel — no apples |

#### Examples:

| German | English | Explanation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Ich habe Hunger.** | I'm hungry. | I have hunger |
| **Ich habe **keinen** Hunger.** | I'm **not** hungry. | I have no hunger |
| **Ich möchte Kaffee.** | I'd like coffee. | |
| **Ich möchte **keinen** Kaffee.** | I'd like **no** coffee. | I don't want coffee |

Note: **kein** changes to **keinen** with masculine nouns (Accusative case)!
We'll learn more about Accusative in a later lesson. For now, focus on the difference between **kein** and **keine**.

### 🆚 Difference between **nicht** and **kein**

| **nicht** | **kein / keine** |
|-----------|------------------|
| Negates the **verb** or **adjective** | Negates the **noun** |
| **Ich esse nicht.** (I don't eat) | **Ich esse kein Fleisch.** (I eat no meat) |
| **Das ist nicht heiß.** (That's not hot) | **Ich habe keine Milch.** (I have no milk) |

#### Comparison sentences:

| German | English | Type |
|--------|---------|:----:|
| **Ich trinke nicht.** | I don't drink. | **nicht** (negates verb) |
| **Ich trinke kein Bier.** | I don't drink beer. | **kein** (negates noun: beer) |
| **Das Brot ist nicht frisch.** | The bread isn't fresh. | **nicht** (negates adjective) |
| **Ich habe keine Bananen.** | I have no bananas. | **keine** (negates noun: bananas) |
| **Er kommt nicht.** | He isn't coming. | **nicht** (negates verb) |

> ⚠️ **Golden Rule:**
> - If there's a **verb** or **adjective** you want to negate → use **nicht** at the end
> - If there's a **noun** you want to say doesn't exist → use **kein/keine** before the noun
> - **kein** with masculine/neuter / **keine** with feminine/plural

## 4. 💬 Conversation (Gespräch) — At the Restaurant

Here is a conversation between **Anna** (customer) and **Kellner** (waiter) at a restaurant:

> **Kellner:** Guten Tag! Was möchten Sie essen?
> *(Good day! What would you like to eat?)*

> **Anna:** Guten Tag! Ich möchte einen Salat, bitte.
> *(Good day! I'd like a salad, please.)*

> **Kellner:** Möchten Sie auch etwas trinken?
> *(Would you also like something to drink?)*

> **Anna:** Ja, ich möchte einen Orangensaft. Haben Sie auch Wasser?
> *(Yes, I'd like an orange juice. Do you also have water?)*

> **Kellner:** Ja, wir haben Wasser mit und ohne Kohlensäure.
> *(Yes, we have sparkling and still water.)*

> **Anna:** Ich möchte Wasser ohne Kohlensäure, bitte.
> *(I'd like still water, please.)*

> **Kellner:** Möchten Sie Fleisch oder Fisch zum Salat?
> *(Would you like meat or fish with the salad?)*

> **Anna:** Nein danke, ich esse **kein** Fleisch und **keinen** Fisch. Ich bin Vegetarierin.
> *(No thanks, I eat no meat and no fish. I'm a vegetarian.)*

> **Kellner:** Kein Problem! Also, ein Mal Salat und einen Orangensaft.
> *(No problem! So, one salad and one orange juice.)*

> **Anna:** Vielen Dank!
> *(Thank you very much!)*

### Questions about the conversation:

| German question | English |
|----------------|---------|
| **Was möchte Anna essen?** | What does Anna want to eat? |
| **Was möchte Anna trinken?** | What does Anna want to drink? |
| **Isst Anna Fleisch?** | Does Anna eat meat? |
| **Warum isst Anna kein Fleisch?** | Why doesn't Anna eat meat? |

### ✅ Answers:

1. Anna möchte **einen Salat** essen.
2. Anna möchte **Orangensaft und Wasser** trinken.
3. Nein, Anna isst **kein** Fleisch.
4. Anna ist **Vegetarierin**.
