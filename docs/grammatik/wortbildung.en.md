# Wortbildung — Word Formation — Prefixes and Suffixes

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Understand how prefixes (Vorsilben) and suffixes (Nachsilben) expand word meaning.
- Apply word‑formation rules to create new, meaningful nouns and verbs.
- Recognise common patterns and avoid typical mistakes.

---

## 📚 Core Rule

In German you can attach **prefixes** before a stem or **suffixes** after a stem to change meaning or word class. The base word often stays unchanged in spelling; only the added element modifies the meaning.

| Type | Example (German) | Meaning | English translation |
|------|-------------------|---------|---------------------|
| **Prefix** (Vorsilbe) | **ent‑** + **kommen** → **entkommen** | to escape | escape |
| **Suffix** (Nachsilbe) | **‑ung** + **Bild** → **Bildung** | education | education |
| **Prefix + Suffix** | **ver‑** + **‑lich** → **ver‑ständig‑lich** | comprehensibly | comprehensibly |

### 💡 Tips

- Not every stem accepts every prefix; some require a vowel change (e.g., **ver‑** often adds an **e** before the suffix).
- Suffixes usually change the word class: **‑ung** turns a verb into a noun, **‑lich** turns a noun/adjective into an adjective.

## 🎯 Tips for Learners

- Keep a list of common prefixes (**ver‑, be‑, ent‑, miss‑**) and suffixes (**‑ung, ‑heit, ‑lich**) to expand your vocabulary.
- Practice forming words with both parts; the meaning often becomes transparent once you know the components.
- Watch out for spelling changes in the stem when a prefix is added (e.g., **ver** often triggers an **e** insertion).
