# Two-Way Prepositions (Wechselpräpositionen) in German

🎯 **Wechselpräpositionen** are prepositions that **change their meaning depending on the case** — with **Dativ** they describe a **location (static)**, with **Akkusativ** they describe a **direction (movement)**.

---

## 💡 List of Two-Way Prepositions

There are **10 two-way prepositions** in German:

| Preposition | Meaning | Example with Dativ (location) | Example with Akkusativ (direction) |
|-------------|---------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **in** | in / into | im (in dem) Haus — inside the house | in das Haus — into the house |
| **auf** | on / onto | auf dem Tisch — on the table | auf den Tisch — onto the table |
| **unter** | under/ underneath | unter dem Bett — under the bed | unter das Bett — to under the bed |
| **über** | over / above | über dem Sofa — above the sofa | über das Sofa — over the sofa |
| **neben** | next to / beside | neben dem Schrank — next to the cupboard | neben den Schrank — to next to the cupboard |
| **zwischen** | between | zwischen den Büchern — between the books | zwischen die Bücher — to between the books |
| **vor** | in front of | vor dem Haus — in front of the house | vor das Haus — to in front of the house |
| **hinter** | behind | hinter der Tür — behind the door | hinter die Tür — to behind the door |
| **an** | on / at / by | an der Wand — on the wall | an die Wand — onto the wall |
| **bei** | at / near | bei der Arbeit — at work | ✗ (rare) |

---

## 🎯 The Golden Rule 🥇

> **Wo?** (Where? — static) → **Dativ** 🟢 — fixed location, something stationary
> **Wohin?** (Where to? — movement) → **Akkusativ** 🔵 — motion, direction, something changing place

### Examples:

| Question | Case | Sentence | Translation |
|----------|------|----------|-------------|
| **Wo** ist die Katze? 🟢 | Dativ | Die Katze ist **auf dem Tisch**. | The cat is on the table (static). |
| **Wohin** springt die Katze? 🔵 | Akkusativ | Die Katze springt **auf den Tisch**. | The cat jumps onto the table (movement). |

---

## ⚠️ Contractions with Articles

When a preposition comes with **der / das**, they often merge:

| Preposition + Article | Contraction |
|----------------------|-------------|
| in + **dem** | **im** |
| in + **das** | **ins** |
| an + **dem** | **am** |
| an + **das** | **ans** |
| auf + **das** | **aufs** (less common) |
| bei + **dem** | **beim** |
| von + **dem** | **vom** |
| zu + **dem** | **zum** |
| zu + **der** | **zur** |

> 📌 **Prepositions that don't contract:** vor, hinter, unter, über, neben, zwischen — stay separate.

## 📊 Table of Meanings with Examples

| Prep. | Dativ (location — Wo?) | Akkusativ (direction — Wohin?) |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| **in** | Ich bin **im** Büro. — I am in the office. | Ich gehe **ins** Büro. — I go into the office. |
| **auf** | Das Buch liegt **auf** dem Tisch. — The book is on the table. | Ich lege das Buch **auf** den Tisch. — I put the book on the table. |
| **unter** | Die Katze sitzt **unter** dem Tisch. — The cat is under the table. | Die Katze läuft **unter** den Tisch. — The cat runs under the table. |
| **über** | Das Bild hängt **über** dem Sofa. — The painting is above the sofa. | Ich hänge das Bild **über** das Sofa. — I hang the painting above the sofa. |
| **neben** | Er steht **neben** mir. — He is standing next to me. | Er setzt sich **neben** mich. — He sits down next to me. |
| **zwischen** | Das Kind sitzt **zwischen** den Eltern. — The child is between the parents. | Das Kind setzt sich **zwischen** die Eltern. — The child sits between the parents. |
| **vor** | Das Auto steht **vor** dem Haus. — The car is in front of the house. | Er fährt **vor** das Haus. — He drives in front of the house. |
| **hinter** | Der Hund ist **hinter** der Tür. — The dog is behind the door. | Der Hund läuft **hinter** die Tür. — The dog runs behind the door. |
| **an** | Das Bild ist **an** der Wand. — The picture is on the wall. | Ich hänge das Bild **an** die Wand. — I hang the picture on the wall. |
| **bei** | Ich bin **beim** Arzt. — I am at the doctor's. | Ich komme **zum** Arzt. — I come to the doctor's. |

| Ich lege das Buch **zwischen die Bücher**. | I put the book between the books (movement). |
| Der Vogel fliegt **über das Haus**. | The bird flies over the house (movement). |
| Der Vogel sitzt **über dem Haus**. | The bird is sitting above the house (static). |

| Die Kinder laufen **in den Garten**. | The children run into the garden. |
| Das Poster hängt **an der Wand**. | The poster is hanging on the wall. |
| Ich hänge das Poster **an die Wand**. | I hang the poster on the wall. |

💡 **Quick tip:** The magic is in **two questions**:
- If the question is **Wo?** → Dativ (location)
- If the question is **Wohin?** → Akkusativ (direction)

Ask yourself these two questions before every use and you'll get it right!
