# Modal Verbs (Modalverben) in German

🎯 **Modalverben** are auxiliary verbs that modify the meaning of the main verb. Instead of saying "I go," you can say "I **can** go" or "I **must** go."

---

## 💡 List of Modal Verbs

German has **6 main modal verbs**:

| Verb | Meaning | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| **können** | can / to be able to | Ability or possibility |
| **müssen** | must / to have to | Obligation or necessity |
| **dürfen** | may / to be allowed to | Permission |
| **sollen** | should / to be supposed to | Recommendation or third-party order |
| **wollen** | to want | Strong desire or intention |
| **mögen / möchte** | to like / would like (polite) | Preference or polite request |

---

## ⚠️ Conjugation in Present Tense (Präsens)

📌 **Important:** Modal verbs have special conjugation — the singular forms (ich, du, er/sie/es) change the vowel (except sollen).

| Pronoun | können | müssen | dürfen | sollen | wollen | mögen | möchte* |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|-------|---------|
| **ich** | kann | muss | darf | soll | will | mag | möchte |
| **du** | kannst | musst | darfst | sollst | willst | magst | möchtest |
| **er/sie/es** | kann | muss | darf | soll | will | mag | möchte |
| **wir** | können | müssen | dürfen | sollen | wollen | mögen | möchten |
| **ihr** | könnt | müsst | dürft | sollt | wollt | mögt | möchtet |
| **sie/Sie** | können | müssen | dürfen | sollen | wollen | mögen | möchten |

> \* `möchte` is originally the Konjunktiv II of mögen, but it is now used as an independent verb meaning "would like" (polite request).

---

## 🎯 Sentence Structure with Modal Verbs

The golden rule 🥇:

> **Modal verb in position 2 — main verb at the end of the sentence (Infinitiv)**

```
[Subject] + [Modalverb (conjugated)] + ... + [Main verb (Infinitiv)]
```

**Example:**
- `Ich **kann** gut Fußball **spielen**.` — I can play soccer well.
- `Er **muss** heute **arbeiten**.` — He has to work today.

---

## ✅ Example Sentences — each verb with translation

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| Ich **kann** Deutsch **sprechen**. | I can speak German. |
| Du **musst** die Hausaufgaben **machen**. | You must do the homework. |
| Hier **darf** man nicht **rauchen**. | Smoking is not allowed here. |
| Sie **soll** mehr Wasser **trinken**. | She should drink more water. |
| Er **will** Arzt **werden**. | He wants to become a doctor. |
| Wir **möchten** einen Kaffee **bestellen**. | We would like to order a coffee. |
| **Kannst** du mir **helfen**? | Can you help me? |
| Die Kinder **wollen** im Park **spielen**. | The children want to play in the park. |
| Ihr **müsst** pünktlich **kommen**. | You (pl.) must come on time. |
| **Darf** ich das Fenster **öffnen**? | May I open the window? |

💡 **Quick tip:** The two most used modal verbs at the beginning are **können** and **müssen**. Start with them, then add the rest gradually. The most important thing — remember: modal verb in position 2, main verb at the end of the sentence!
