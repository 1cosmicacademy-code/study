## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Understand how location, direction and origin are expressed with German prepositions.
- Distinguish between **wo?**, **wohin?** and **woher?** in everyday sentences.
- Apply the prepositions correctly in speaking and writing.

---

## 📚 Core rules

| Preposition | Question it answers | Example question |
|-------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| **wo?** | *where?* (static location) | **Wo** bist du? — Where are you?
| **wohin?** | *where to?* (direction of movement) | **Wohin** gehst du? — Where are you going?
| **woher?** | *where from?* (origin) | **Woher** kommst du? — Where do you come from?

### Usage notes

- **wo?** is used when the subject stays in place. It never implies movement.
- **wohin?** is paired with verbs of motion (*gehen, fahren, laufen, fliegen*). It asks for the destination.
- **woher?** asks for the source or origin. Often used with *kommen, stammen, beziehen*.
- If the verb does not denote motion, use **wo?** even if the sentence talks about a change of state (e.g., *Der Zug steht **wo**?* vs *Der Zug fährt **wohin**?*).
- The three questions can be combined: *Wo bist du? Wohin willst du gehen? Woher kommst du?*

---

## 📝 Sample sentences

- **Wo** ist das Buch? — Where is the book? ✅
- **Wohin** fährst du? — Where are you traveling to? ✅
- **Woher** kommt er? — Where does he come from? ✅
- **Wo** liegt die Stadt? — Where is the city located? ✅
- **Wohin** willst du gehen? — Where do you want to go? ✅
