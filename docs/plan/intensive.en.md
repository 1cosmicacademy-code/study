# ⚡ Intensive Track — Focused and Consistent

> **Pace:** 3 lessons/week | **Study days:** 6 days/week | **Hours:** 12 hours/week
>
> **A1: 8 weeks ← A2: 8 weeks ← B1: 6 weeks ← Total: ~5.5 months**

An intensive track for those who want quick progress while maintaining learning quality. Requires 6 days per week but cuts the timeline by ~30% compared to the Smart track.

---

## 🎯 Who Is This Track For?

| Category | Fit |
|:---------|:----:|
| 👨‍💼 **Employee with stable daily routine** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| 🎓 **Student on summer break** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| 🛫 **Has a deadline (travel, work, study)** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| 🧠 **Fast learner** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| 🔄 **Has studied German before and is reviewing** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |

Suitable if you're **motivated and committed** and can dedicate 2 hours daily for 6 days a week, aiming to complete A1→B1 in under 6 months.

---

## ✅ Pros

- ⏱️ **30% faster than Smart** — Saves ~2.5 months
- 📅 **Consistent daily routine** — Studying becomes a daily habit
- 🧠 **Faster retention** — Daily repetition strengthens long-term memory
- 🎯 **High learning momentum** — No long gaps between lessons
- 🔄 **Balanced distribution** — 3 lessons + grammar + review = complete coverage without exhaustion

## ❌ Cons

- 🔥 **Burnout risk** — 6 days a week can be draining long-term
- 📆 **High daily commitment** — Hard to maintain with unexpected circumstances
- 🚫 **Small margin for error** — Missing a week = losing 3 full lessons
- 💪 **Requires high discipline** — No room for procrastination

---

## 📅 Weekly Schedule

### Weekly Rhythm Overview

| Day | Duration | Task |
|:---:|:--------:|------|
| 📅 Monday | 2 hours | 📕 **Lesson 1** + Übungsbuch exercises |
| 📅 Tuesday | 2 hours | 📕 **Lesson 2** + Übungsbuch exercises |
| 📅 Wednesday | 2 hours | 📘 **Grammar for both lessons** (L1+L2) + vocabulary review |
| 📅 Thursday | 2 hours | 📕 **Lesson 3** + Übungsbuch exercises |
| 📅 Friday | 2 hours | 📝 **Review & extra exercises** + word repetition |
| 📅 Saturday | 2 hours | 📘 **Grammar for Lesson 3** + **weekly test** |
| 🎯 Sunday | — | **Rest** |

### Time Breakdown per Day

**Lesson Day (Monday/Tuesday/Thursday):**
```
📕 2 hours = 120 minutes
 ├── 🎬 Lesson video                   →  10 min
 ├── 📖 Read explanations & tables     →  35 min
 ├── 💬 Dialogue & helper sentences    →  15 min
 ├── 📝 Interactive Übungsbuch exercises → 30 min
 ├── 🔍 Review solutions                →  15 min
 └── 🗂️ Learn new vocabulary           →  15 min
```

**Grammar Day (Wednesday):**
```
📘 2 hours = 120 minutes
 ├── 📘 Grammar for Lesson 1            →  25 min
 ├── 📘 Grammar for Lesson 2            →  25 min
 ├── ✍️ Interactive grammar exercises   →  30 min
 ├── 🔄 Review week's vocabulary        →  15 min
 └── 📝 Notes and questions             →  15 min
```

**Review Day (Friday):**
```
📝 2 hours = 120 minutes
 ├── 🔄 Repeat all 3 lessons of the week →  30 min
 ├── 📝 Extra exercises (optional)       →  30 min
 ├── 🗣️ Practical application            →  20 min
 ├── 📱 Vocabulary flashcards            →  20 min
 └── ❓ Questions & clarifications       →  20 min
```

**Weekly Test Day (Saturday):**
```
📘 2 hours = 120 minutes
 ├── 📘 Grammar for Lesson 3            →  25 min
 ├── ✍️ Grammar exercises               →  20 min
 ├── 📝 Weekly test                     →  25 min
 ├── 🔍 Correction & error analysis     →  20 min
 └── 📅 Plan next week                  →  10 min
```

## 💡 Intensive Track Tips

1. **⏰ Fixed timing** — Set a consistent 2-hour slot daily (e.g., 7-9 PM) to build a habit
2. **📝 Journal daily** — Write 3 things you learned each day — this solidifies the material
3. **🎯 Wednesday & Saturday are consolidation days** — Don't neglect grammar; it's the backbone of the language
4. **💧 Take Sunday as real rest** — Your brain needs time to process information
5. **🔁 If you miss a day** — Continue from the next day, don't cram 3 lessons in one day
6. **🗣️ Friday is application day** — Try speaking or writing in German on review day
