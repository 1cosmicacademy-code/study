## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Understand how to build the past passive (Perfekt) in German.
- Recognise the structure **sein + Participle II + von + agent**.
- Apply the form to everyday sentences and avoid typical errors.

---

## 📚 Core Rule

**Past Passive (Perfekt)** is formed with the auxiliary **sein**, the **Partizip II** of the main verb, and optionally the preposition **von** plus the agent (the person who performed the action).

Structure:
```
<subject> + ist/war + Partizip II + (von + agent)
```
If the sentence is in pluperfect, use **war** + **Partizip II** + **worden**.

| Tense | Auxiliary | Example | Translation |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Perfekt | ist | Das Buch **ist** von dem Autor **geschrieben**. | The book **was written** by the author. |
| Präteritum | war | Der Brief **war** von ihr **geschickt** worden. | The letter **had been sent** by her. |

---

## ✅ Example Sentences

| German sentence | Arabic translation |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Das Haus **wurde** von den Nachbarn **gebaut**. | تم بناء البيت من الجيار.
| Der Kuchen **ist** von meiner Mutter **gebacken**. | الكك محتوي من أمي.
| Die Aufgabe **war** von uns **gelöst** worden. | كانت مهمة محلولة بنا.

## ملاحظات للمتعلمين

- The passive does **not** change the case of objects; the original object becomes the subject.
- The agent can be omitted when it is obvious or not important.
- In perfect passive, the past participle is **always** preceded by **sein** (not **haben**).
- Remember the difference between **wurde** (simple past passive) and **ist ... geworden** (perfect passive).
