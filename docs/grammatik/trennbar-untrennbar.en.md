# Trennbare und untrennbare Verben — Separable and inseparable verbs

[🔙 Back to Grammar](../index.md)

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Distinguish between separable prefixes and inseparable verbs.
- Apply the rule in present, perfect and other tenses.
- Avoid common pitfalls when forming sentences.

---

## 📚 Core Rule

**Separable verbs** (Trennbare Verben) have a prefix that splits off from the verb stem in the finite verb position (present tense, imperative, past tense with *sein/haben* in perfect). The prefix moves to the end of the clause.

**Inseparable verbs** (Untrennbare Verben) keep their prefix attached to the stem in all tenses; the verb behaves as a single word.

### Examples

```
Ich rufe dich an. → rufe (verb) + an (separable prefix)
Er ruft ___ an? (question)
```

```
Ich verstehe das Problem nicht.
(verb "verstehen" – inseparable, no split)
```

### Common Prefixes (Separable)
| Prefix | Example | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **ab-** | **abfahren** | to depart |
| **an-** | **anrufen** | to call |
| **auf-** | **aufstehen** | to stand up |
| **aus-** | **ausgehen** | to go out |

### Common Inseparable Prefixes
| Prefix | Example | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **ver-** | **verstehen** | to understand |
| **be-** | **bekommen** | to receive |
| **er-** | **erklären** | to explain |
| **ge-** | **gebären** | to give birth |

## 🟡 Tips for Learners

- Do **not** split verbs that start with inseparable prefixes such as **ver-**, **be-**, **er-**.
- In the perfect tense with separable verbs, the prefix stays attached to the past participle and the **haben/sein** auxiliary comes before it (e.g., *Ich habe angerufen*).
- Keep a list of the most common separable prefixes (**an-, auf-, aus-, ein-, mit-, nach-, vor-, zu-**) to quickly recognise them.
