# 📕 Lektion 6: Arbeitszeiten — Working Hours and Time

> 🕐 **Welcome!** In this lesson you'll learn how to ask for the time, tell the time, and talk about working hours in German. This is a very important lesson because Germans are known for being precise about time!

## 1. 🕐 New Words (Neue Wörter)

First, you need to know the basic time words:

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **die Uhr** | **dee oor** | the clock / watch |
| **die Uhrzeit** | **dee OOR-tsyt** | the time (what time it is) |
| **die Stunde** | **dee SHTOON-duh** | the hour (duration) |
| **die Minute** | **dee mi-NOO-tuh** | the minute |
| **die Sekunde** | **dee ze-KOON-duh** | the second |
| **spät** | **shpayt** | late |
| **pünktlich** | **PUNKT-lish** | punctual / on time |
| **früh** | **fröö** | early |
| **die Arbeit** | **dee AR-byt** | the work |
| **die Pause** | **dee POW-zuh** | the break |
| **frei** | **fry** | free / day off |
| **die Zeit** | **dee tsyt** | the time |

> 💡 **Tip:** The word **Uhr** has two meanings in German: (1) the clock or watch, and (2) you use it when telling the time. Example: **Es ist 8 Uhr.** = It's 8 o'clock.

---

## 2. ⏰ How to Tell Time (Die Uhrzeit)

In German, there are two ways to tell the time. Focus on the first way first — it's the easiest!

### 🟢 Method 1 — The Easy Way (Official + 24-hour)

In Germany, people mostly use the 24-hour system (especially for appointments and work). You say the hour + **Uhr** + the minutes.

| Time | In German | Pronunciation |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 08:00 | **Acht Uhr** | akht oor |
| 09:00 | **Neun Uhr** | noyn oor |
| 12:00 | **Zwölf Uhr** | tsvelf oor |
| 13:00 | **Dreizehn Uhr** | DRAI-tsayn oor |
| 15:00 | **Fünfzehn Uhr** | FÜNFF-tsayn oor |
| 18:00 | **Achtzehn Uhr** | AKHT-tsayn oor |
| 20:00 | **Zwanzig Uhr** | TSVAHN-tsikh oor |

### With minutes:

| Time | In German | Pronunciation |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 08:15 | **Acht Uhr fünfzehn** | akht oor FÜNFF-tsayn |
| 09:30 | **Neun Uhr dreißig** | noyn oor DRAI-sikh |
| 10:45 | **Zehn Uhr fünfundvierzig** | tsayn oor fünff-unt-FEER-tsikh |
| 14:05 | **Vierzehn Uhr fünf** | FEER-tsayn oor fünff |
| 16:20 | **Sechzehn Uhr zwanzig** | ZEKH-tsayn oor TSVAHN-tsikh |

### 🟡 Method 2 — Common (Informal)

In everyday conversation, Germans split the hour into half and quarter parts:

| Time | In German (informal) | Explanation |
|------|---------------------|-------------|
| 08:00 | **Acht Uhr** | 8 o'clock sharp |
| 08:05 | **fünf nach acht** | 5 minutes **after** eight |
| 08:15 | **Viertel nach acht** | **Quarter** past eight |
| 08:30 | **halb neun** | **Half** nine (halfway to nine) |
| 08:45 | **Viertel vor neun** | **Quarter** to nine |
| 08:50 | **zehn vor neun** | 10 **to** nine |

> ⚠️ **Warning!** In German, **halb neun** means 8:30 (halfway to 9), NOT 9:30! This is a very common mistake. HALB means halfway to the next hour.

### 📌 Useful time words:

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| **nach** | past / after |
| **vor** | to / before |
| **Viertel** | quarter |
| **halb** | half |
| **genau** | exactly / sharp |
| **ungefähr** | approximately |
| **gegen** | around (a bit before) |
| **um** | at (with specific time) |

### Examples:
- **um 8 Uhr** — at 8 o'clock
- **um halb zehn** — at 9:30
- **gegen 3 Uhr** — around 3 o'clock

## 3. 📅 Days of the Week (Die Wochentage)

When talking about work hours, you need the days of the week:

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Montag** | **MOHN-tahk** | Monday |
| **Dienstag** | **DEENS-tahk** | Tuesday |
| **Mittwoch** | **MIT-vokh** | Wednesday |
| **Donnerstag** | **DON-ners-tahk** | Thursday |
| **Freitag** | **FRY-tahk** | Friday |
| **Samstag** | **ZAHM-stahk** | Saturday |
| **Sonntag** | **ZOHN-tahk** | Sunday |
| **der Tag** | **deer tahk** | the day |
| **die Woche** | **dee VOKH-uh** | the week |
| **das Wochenende** | **dahs VOKH-en-en-duh** | the weekend |
| **am** (an + dem) | **ahm** | on (a day) — e.g. am Montag |

> **Rule:** All days of the week are **masculine (der)** in German. Say **am Montag** = on Monday.

---

## 4. 📝 Important Grammar: Time Prepositions (Präpositionen)

### um — at (for a specific time)

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Der Unterricht beginnt um 9 Uhr.** | Class starts at 9 o'clock. |
| **Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf.** | I wake up at 7 o'clock. |
| **Die Arbeit endet um 17 Uhr.** | Work ends at 5 PM. |

### von … bis — from … to (for a time period)

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Ich arbeite von 8 bis 17 Uhr.** | I work from 8 to 5. |
| **Die Pause ist von 12 bis 13 Uhr.** | The break is from 12 to 1. |
| **Der Kurs dauert von Montag bis Freitag.** | The course runs from Monday to Friday. |

### am — on (for a day of the week)

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Am Montag habe ich Unterricht.** | On Monday I have class. |
| **Am Samstag arbeite ich nicht.** | On Saturday I don't work. |
| **Am Wochenende bin ich frei.** | On the weekend I'm free. |

> 🎯 **Summary:**
> - **um** + time = at (a specific time)
> - **von** + time **bis** + time = from...to...
> - **am** + day = on (a specific day)

## 5. 💬 Conversation (Gespräch) — At Work

Here is a conversation between **Herr Müller** (the boss) and **Frau Schmidt** (the employee) talking about work hours.

> **Herr Müller:** Guten Morgen, Frau Schmidt! Wie spät ist es?
> *(Good morning, Mrs. Schmidt! What time is it?)*

> **Frau Schmidt:** Guten Morgen, Herr Müller! Es ist Viertel nach acht.
> *(Good morning, Mr. Müller! It's quarter past eight.)*

> **Herr Müller:** Danke. Wann beginnt die Arbeit?
> *(Thanks. When does work start?)*

> **Frau Schmidt:** Die Arbeit beginnt um 8 Uhr. Das ist jetzt.
> *(Work starts at 8 o'clock. That's now.)*

> **Herr Müller:** Ah ja! Und wann ist die Pause?
> *(Ah yes! And when is the break?)*

> **Frau Schmidt:** Die Pause ist von 12 Uhr bis 13 Uhr.
> *(The break is from 12 o'clock to 1 o'clock.)*

> **Herr Müller:** Und wann endet die Arbeit?
> *(And when does work end?)*

> **Frau Schmidt:** Die Arbeit endet um 17 Uhr.
> *(Work ends at 5 PM.)*

> **Herr Müller:** Perfekt! Ich bin pünktlich. Vielen Dank!
> *(Perfect! I'm on time. Thank you very much!)*

> **Frau Schmidt:** Bitte sehr! Um 17 Uhr sehen wir uns wieder.
> *(You're welcome! At 5 PM we'll see each other again.)*

### Important questions from the conversation:

| German question | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| **Wie spät ist es?** | **vee shpayt ist es** | What time is it? |
| **Wann beginnt die Arbeit?** | **van be-GINT dee AR-byt** | When does work start? |
| **Wann endet die Arbeit?** | **van EN-det dee AR-byt** | When does work end? |
| **Wann ist die Pause?** | **van ist dee POW-zuh** | When is the break? |
| **Um wie viel Uhr?** | **oom vee feel oor** | At what time? |

## 6. ⏳ Duration: How long does it take? (Dauer)

When asking about duration, use these sentences:

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Wie lange dauert das?** | **vee LAHNG-uh DOW-ert dahs** | How long does that take? |
| **Das dauert eine Stunde.** | **dahs DOW-ert EYE-nuh SHTOON-duh** | That takes one hour. |
| **Das dauert zwei Stunden.** | **dahs DOW-ert tsvai SHTOON-den** | That takes two hours. |
| **eine halbe Stunde** | **EYE-nuh HAL-buh SHTOON-duh** | half an hour |
| **eine Viertelstunde** | **EYE-nuh FEER-tel-SHTOON-duh** | a quarter of an hour |
| **drei Tage** | **drai TAH-guh** | three days |
| **eine Woche** | **EYE-nuh VOKH-uh** | one week |

### Examples:
- **Der Film dauert zwei Stunden.** — The movie lasts two hours.
- **Die Pause dauert 30 Minuten.** — The break lasts 30 minutes.
- **Der Kurs dauert 6 Wochen.** — The course lasts 6 weeks.
