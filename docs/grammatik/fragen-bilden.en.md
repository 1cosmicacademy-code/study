## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Learn how to build **W‑questions** (who, what, where, etc.) in German.
- Understand the structure of **yes/no questions**.
- Practice turning statements into questions for everyday conversations.

---

## 📚 Core Rules

### 1️⃣ **W‑Questions**

A **W‑question** starts with an interrogative word, followed by the verb in **verb‑first** order, then the subject and the rest of the sentence.

| German interrogative | Arabic meaning |
|----------------------|----------------|
| **Wer?** (who) | من؟ |
| **Was?** (what) | ما؟ |
| **Wo?** (where) | أين؟ |
| **Wann?** (when) | متى؟ |
| **Wie?** (how) | كيف؟ |
| **Warum?** (why) | لماذا؟ |
| **Wohin?** (to where) | إلى أين؟ |
| **Woher?** (from where) | من أين؟ |

**Structure**: `W‑word + Verb + Subject + …`

*Example*: **Wer bist du?** – *Who are you?*

### 2️⃣ **Yes/No Questions**

Simply place the **finite verb** at the beginning of the sentence, then follow with the subject and the rest.

- If the main verb is an auxiliary (**sein, haben, modal verbs**), it stays at the front.
- No inversion of subject and verb is needed beyond this first‑verb position.

*Examples*:
- **Bist du müde?** – *Are you tired?*
- **Hast du Zeit?** – *Do you have time?*
- **Kann er kommen?** – *Can he come?*

## ✅ Summary

- **W‑questions**: `W‑word + Verb + Subject …`
- **Yes/No questions**: `Verb + Subject + …`
- Practice daily by turning statements you hear into questions.
