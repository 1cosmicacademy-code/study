## 🎯 Lesson goals

- Practice using common adverbs in everyday sentences.

## 📚 Core rule

Präpositionaladverbs are words that stand in for a prepositional phrase. They are built from the stem **da** (or **dar**) or **wo** plus the original preposition. Use **da‑** when the phrase refers to a definite object ("that"), and **wo‑** when it refers to a location or a question.

| Basic preposition | with **da(r)** | with **wo(r)** |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------|
| darauf            | da(r) + darauf | wo(r) + darauf |
| darüber           | da(r) + darüber| wo(r) + darüber |
| damit             | da(r) + damit  | wo(r) + damit |
| davon             | da(r) + davon  | wo(r) + davon |
| womit             | wo(m) + womit   | wo(r) + womit |

### Typical pitfalls
- **da‑** is used when the reference is definite ("that"), e.g., *Ich warte darauf* (I wait for that).
- **wo‑** is used for questions or non‑specific references, e.g., *Womit fährst du?* (With what are you traveling?).
- In some cases **da‑** becomes **dar‑** before a vowel‑starting preposition (e.g., *darauf*).

---

### Examples

- **Ich warte darauf.** – I am waiting for that.
- **Womit fährst du?** – With what are you traveling?
- **Er spricht darüber.** – He talks about it.
- **Sie denkt daran.** – She thinks about that.
- **Wir freuen uns darüber.** – We are happy about it.
