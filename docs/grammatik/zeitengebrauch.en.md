# Tense usage (Tempus) — Using German tenses (A2)

[🔙 Back to grammar index](../index.md)

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Identify the main German tenses and when to use each.
- Link each tense to a typical time reference (now, past, future).
- Practice forming sentences in every tense.

---

## 📚 Core rules

| Tense | Typical time reference | Example sentence |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Präsens** (Present) | actions happening now or habitual actions | **Ich lese** das Buch. — I am reading the book. |
| **Perfekt** (Present perfect) | completed actions in the recent past (have/has) | **Ich habe** das Buch gelesen. — I have read the book. |
| **Präteritum** (Simple past) | narrative past, written language, or simple statements | **Er ging** zur Schule. — He went to school. |
| **Futur I** (Future I) | future intention or prediction using *werden* + infinitive | **Ich werde** einen Brief schreiben. — I will write a letter. |
| **Futur II** (Future perfect) | action that will be completed before another future point | **Ich werde** die Arbeit beendet haben. — I will have finished the work. |

### Usage notes

- **Präsens** can also refer to the near future when a time expression is added (e.g., *Morgen gehe ich...*).
- **Perfekt** is the most common past tense in spoken German; **Präteritum** is used mainly in writing and for modal verbs.
- **Futur I** is often replaced by **Präsens** with a future adverb, but it is useful for clear future reference.
- **Futur II** is rare in daily speech; it shows that something will be finished before another future event.

---

## 📝 Sample sentences

- **Präsens**: *Ich lerne gerade Deutsch.* — I am currently learning German. ✅
- **Perfekt**: *Wir haben das Spiel gewonnen.* — We have won the game. ✅
- **Präteritum**: *Sie sah den Film gestern.* — She saw the movie yesterday. ✅
- **Futur I**: *Morgen werde ich schwimmen gehen.* — Tomorrow I will go swimming. ✅
- **Futur II**: *Bis 2025 wird er das Projekt abgeschlossen haben.* — By 2025 he will have completed the project. ✅
