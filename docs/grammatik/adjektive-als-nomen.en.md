## 📚 Core Rule

When an adjective is used as a noun it takes a definite article and the gender that belongs to the adjective’s meaning:
- **Neuter** for abstract qualities (e.g., *das Gute* – the good).
- **Masculine** for male persons or professions (e.g., *der Alte* – the old man).
- **Feminine** for female persons or professions (e.g., *die Kleine* – the little girl).

| Case | Example | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Neuter | **das Gute** (the good) | "good" used as a noun, neuter
| Masculine | **der Alte** (the old man) | "old" used as a noun, masculine
| Feminine | **die Kleine** (the little girl) | "little" used as a noun, feminine |

### Examples

- **das Schöne** – "the beautiful thing"
- **der Deutsche** – "the German (person)"
- **die Kleine** – "the little one (female)"
