# Comparisons (Vergleiche) in German

🎯 **Comparisons** allow us to compare two or more things. In German, we use **wie** for equality and **als** for difference. Additionally, adjectives take special forms for the comparative and superlative.

---

## 💡 When do we use wie and when als?

### 1. wie — like / as (Equality)

We use **wie** when two things are equal or similar.

```
Er ist so groß wie ich.
He is as tall as me.
```

```
Sie singt so schön wie ihre Mutter.
She sings as beautifully as her mother.
```

> ⚠️ **Note:** Before **wie** you often find **so** or **genauso** (just as).

### 2. als — than (Difference)

We use **als** when there is a difference between the two things.

```
Er ist größer als ich.
He is taller than me.
```

```
Deutsch ist schwerer als Englisch.
German is more difficult than English.
```

---

## ⚠️ The Rule: Adjective Forms for Comparison

### 🟢 Positive Form (Positiv)

The basic form of the adjective:
- **groß** — big / tall
- **klein** — small
- **schnell** — fast
- **schön** — beautiful
- **teuer** — expensive
- **gut** — good
- **gern** — gladly

### 🟢 Comparative (Komparativ)

Adjective + **-er** (meaning "more ... than"):

| Basic Adjective | Comparative (Komparativ) | Meaning |
|:--------------:|:-----------------------:|:--------:|
| **groß** | **größer** | bigger / taller |
| **klein** | **kleiner** | smaller |
| **schnell** | **schneller** | faster |
| **schön** | **schöner** | more beautiful |
| **teuer** | **teurer** | more expensive |
| **billig** | **billiger** | cheaper |
| **langsam** | **langsamer** | slower |

> ⚠️ Important notes:
> - Words with a, o, u often take an **Umlaut** (ä, ö, ü): groß → **größer**
> - Words ending in **er** drop the e: teuer → **teurer**
> - Words ending in **el** drop the e: dunkel → **dunkler**

### 🟢 Superlative (Superlativ)

Two forms:

1. **am + adjective + -(e)sten** (when not mentioning the noun):
   - **am größten** — the biggest/tallest
   - **am schnellsten** — the fastest

2. **der/die/das + adjective + -(e)ste** (when mentioning the noun):
   - **der größte Mann** — the tallest man
   - **die schnellste Frau** — the fastest woman

| Basic Adjective | Komparativ | Superlativ (am ...) |
|:--------------:|:----------:|:------------------:|
| **groß** | **größer** | **am größten** |
| **schnell** | **schneller** | **am schnellsten** |
| **schön** | **schöner** | **am schönsten** |
| **jung** | **jünger** | **am jüngsten** |
| **alt** | **älter** | **am ältesten** |

### 🟢 Irregular Forms

| Basic Adjective | Komparativ | Superlativ |
|:--------------:|:----------:|:----------:|
| **gut** (good) | **besser** | **am besten** |
| **viel** (much) | **mehr** | **am meisten** |
| **gern** (gladly) | **lieber** | **am liebsten** |
| **hoch** (high) | **höher** | **am höchsten** |
| **nah** (near) | **näher** | **am nächsten** |

## ✅ Example Sentences with Translation

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|------------|
| Ich bin so alt wie du. | I am the same age as you. |
| Er läuft schneller als sein Bruder. | He runs faster than his brother. |
| Wien ist schöner als Berlin? | Is Vienna more beautiful than Berlin? |
| Das Auto ist teurer als das Fahrrad. | The car is more expensive than the bicycle. |
| Deutsch ist am schwersten. | German is the most difficult. |
| Der Mount Everest ist der höchste Berg der Welt. | Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world. |
| Ich esse am liebsten Pizza. | I like eating pizza the most. |
| Sie ist die beste Schülerin in der Klasse. | She is the best student in the class. |
