## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Identify the four common German particles: **doch**, **mal**, **ja**, **eben**.
- Understand their pragmatic functions in everyday sentences.
- Avoid typical mistakes when using them.

---

## 📚 Core Rule

The particles **doch**, **mal**, **ja**, **eben** are *modal particles* (Modalpartikeln). They do not change the grammatical structure but add nuance, attitude, or emphasis. Use them according to the meaning they convey, not as interchangeable substitutes.

| Particle | Function | Example (German) | Translation (English) |
|----------|----------|------------------|-----------------------|
| **doch** | Reinforces an expectation that is contrary to reality or emphasizes a suggestion. | *Komm doch mit!* | "Do come along!" (with a gentle urging)
| **mal**   | Softens a command, makes it sound more casual or brief. | *Komm mal her!* | "Come here for a moment."
| **ja**    | Highlights that something is obvious or self‑evident. | *Das ist ja klar.* | "That's obvious, of course."
| **eben**  | Indicates that a situation is exactly as described, often resigning to a fact. | *So ist es eben.* | "That's just the way it is."

### Typical Errors (Typische Fehler)
- **doch** is *not* a synonym for **aber**; using it as a conjunction creates a semantic error.
- **mal** should not be used to add a temporal meaning (that would be **einmal**).
- **ja** does not replace **nicht wahr** or **oder**; it merely stresses obviousness.
- **eben** is not interchangeable with **schon**; **eben** stresses that something is exactly as stated.

---

## 🎯 Example Sentences

1. *Du hast doch bestimmt Zeit?* — "You surely have time, don’t you?"
2. *Mach das mal bitte!* — "Do that, please, just for a moment."
3. *Das Wetter ist ja schön.* — "The weather is indeed nice."
4. *Er ist eben zu spät gekommen.* — "He arrived late, just like that."
