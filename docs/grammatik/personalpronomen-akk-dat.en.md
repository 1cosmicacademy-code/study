# Personal Pronouns (Personalpronomen) in Akkusativ and Dativ — mich, dir, ihm

[🔙 Back to Grammar](index.md)

---

## 💡 Why Do We Need Pronouns in Akkusativ and Dativ?

In German sentences, personal pronouns change depending on their **grammatical function**:
- **Nominativ** = subject (ich, du, er...)
- **Akkusativ** = direct object (mich, dich, ihn...)
- **Dativ** = indirect object (mir, dir, ihm...)

---

## 📊 Complete Comparison Table

| Nominativ | ➡️ Akkusativ | ➡️ Dativ |
|:---------:|:------------:|:--------:|
| **ich** | **mich** (me) | **mir** (to me) |
| **du** | **dich** (you) | **dir** (to you) |
| **er** | **ihn** (him) | **ihm** (to him) |
| **sie** | **sie** (her) | **ihr** (to her) |
| **es** | **es** (it) | **ihm** (to it) |
| **wir** | **uns** (us) | **uns** (to us) |
| **ihr** | **euch** (you) | **euch** (to you) |
| **sie** | **sie** (them) | **ihnen** (to them) |
| **Sie** | **Sie** (you) | **Ihnen** (to you) |

---

## 🔴 When to Use Akkusativ?

**Akkusativ** = direct object (the person/thing the action happens to):

- Ich liebe **dich**. — I love you.
- Er sieht **mich**. — He sees me.
- Wir besuchen **ihn** morgen. — We are visiting him tomorrow.
- Kannst du **sie** verstehen? — Can you understand her?
- Ich rufe **euch** an. — I'm calling you.

### Prepositions with Akkusativ

Some prepositions always take Akkusativ:

| Preposition | Example | Translation |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **für** + Akk | für **dich** | for you |
| **ohne** + Akk | ohne **mich** | without me |
| **durch** + Akk | durch **uns** | through us |
| **gegen** + Akk | gegen **ihn** | against him |

---

## 🔵 When to Use Dativ?

**Dativ** = indirect object (the person for whom something is done):

- Ich helfe **dir**. — I help you. (I give help *to you*)
- Er dankt **mir**. — He thanks me.
- Wir gehören zu **ihnen**. — We belong to them.
- Wie geht es **Ihnen**? — How are you? (formal)
- Ich schenke **ihr** Blumen. — I give her flowers.

### Prepositions with Dativ

Some prepositions always take Dativ:

| Preposition | Example | Translation |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **mit** + Dat | mit **dir** | with you |
| **von** + Dat | von **mir** | from me |
| **zu** + Dat | zu **uns** | to us |
| **bei** + Dat | bei **ihm** | at his place |
| **aus** + Dat | aus **mir** | from within me |
| **nach** + Dat | nach **ihr** | after her |

---

## ⚠️ Difference Between Akkusativ and Dativ with the Same Verb

Some verbs take two objects (Akkusativ + Dativ):

| Verb | Akkusativ (what?) | Dativ (to whom?) | Example |
|------|:-----------------:|:----------------:|---------|
| **geben** (give) | the thing given | the recipient | Ich gebe **dir** das Buch. |
| **schenken** (present) | the gift | the receiver | Er schenkt **mir** Blumen. |
| **zeigen** (show) | the thing | the person | Zeig **ihm** den Weg! |
| **sagen** (tell) | the message | the listener | Sag **mir** die Wahrheit! |
| **schicken** (send) | the thing | the recipient | Ich schicke **euch** die Einladung. |
