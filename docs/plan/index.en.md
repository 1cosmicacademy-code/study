# 🗓️ Study Plan — Choose Your Track

> A plan based on the actual site content: 40 lessons + 70 grammar topics + interactive videos.
> All numbers here are calculated precisely — no floating estimates.

Choose the track that fits your time and commitments. Every track takes you from A1 to B1, but at a different pace:

---

## 🚀 Smart Track — Balanced and Sustainable

> **Pace:** 2 lessons/week | **4 days/week** | **8 hours/week** | **~8 months**

**Recommended** for most learners. Combines consistency with flexibility, suitable for those with work or study commitments while maintaining steady learning momentum.

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|:--------|:--------|
| High flexibility — 4 days only + 3 rest days | Slowest track — ~8 months to B1 |
| Better retention — day between lesson sessions | Requires self-discipline |
| High completion rate — no burnout | Long breaks can affect momentum |
| Matches Goethe-Institut pace at no cost | |

**🎯 Ideal for:** Full-time employee, university student, beginner in German, family/parent

[→ **Learn more about the Smart Track**](smart.en.md)

---

## ⚡ Intensive Track — Focused and Consistent

> **Pace:** 3 lessons/week | **6 days/week** | **12 hours/week** | **~5.5 months**

**30% faster** than the Smart track. Requires 6 days per week but significantly cuts the timeline. Suitable for those with a stable daily routine.

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|:--------|:--------|
| 30% faster — saves ~2.5 months | Burnout risk — 6 days a week |
| Consistent daily routine — studying becomes a habit | High daily commitment — hard with unexpected events |
| Faster retention — daily repetition strengthens memory | Small margin for error — missing a week = losing 3 lessons |
| Balanced distribution — 3 lessons + grammar + review | Requires high discipline |

**🎯 Ideal for:** Student on summer break, learner with a deadline (travel/work/study), fast learner

[→ **Learn more about the Intensive Track**](intensive.en.md)

---

## 🔥 Turbo Track — Maximum Speed

> **Pace:** 5 lessons/week | **6 days/week** | **18 hours/week** | **~3.5 months**

An **ultra-intensive** track for those who want to master German in the shortest possible time. Requires near-full-time commitment and iron discipline.

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|:--------|:--------|
| Fastest track — A1→B1 in ~3.5 months only | Very high burnout risk |
| Full immersion — German becomes part of your life | Near-full-time commitment needed |
| Unstoppable momentum — tangible progress every week | No margin for error — missing 3 days = losing 2 lessons |
| Quick achievement — strong psychological motivation | Little consolidation time — information piles up too fast |

> ⚠️ This track is **not for complete beginners** in self-study.

**🎯 Ideal for:** Traveling to Germany in 3-4 months, full-time student, relocating for work

[→ **Learn more about the Turbo Track**](turbo.en.md)

## 🧮 How We Calculated Actual Time

Accurate breakdown of every site component:

```
📕 One A1 Lesson (Kursbuch):
   ├── 🎬 Watch lesson video (YouTube, 5-10 min)
   ├── 📖 Read explanation + tables (~7 word tables)  →  30-40 min
   ├── 💬 Read dialogue + understand                   →  10-15 min
   └── 📝 Helper sentences and phrases                 →   5-10 min
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total: 45-60 minutes

📝 Übungsbuch Exercises (per lesson):
   ├── Interactive exercises (4-6 with instant grading) →  25-35 min
   └── Review solutions and mistakes                   →  10-15 min
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total: 40-50 minutes

📘 Related Grammar (per lesson):
   ├── Read grammar page (tables + explanation)        →  15-20 min
   └── Solve interactive exercises (3-5)               →  15-20 min
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Total: 35-45 minutes

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Total per complete lesson:           ~1.5 - 2 hours
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

> 📐 **Integrated methodology:** This program combines the lesson, exercises, and related grammar into one integrated weekly unit. This approach ensures better reinforcement and time efficiency, rather than spreading components across separate weeks which disrupts learning continuity.

## 💡 Golden Tips for Time Optimization

1. **🎬 Video first** — Watch the lesson video before reading (5-10 min gives you the context)
2. **📝 Exercises right away** — Solve Übungsbuch right after the lesson (information is fresh)
3. **⏱️ Time yourself** — Use a timer: 45 min for lesson, 30 min for exercises, 20 min for grammar
4. **🔁 Don't master Day 1** — Return to difficult words the next day (spaced repetition)
5. **📱 Flashcards** — New words need 5-7 repetitions to stick
6. **🗣️ Speak out loud** — Even if alone — German needs muscle practice
7. **✅ Interactive exercises** — Take advantage of instant correction
8. **🎯 Review every 5 lessons** — Don't wait until the end of the level
