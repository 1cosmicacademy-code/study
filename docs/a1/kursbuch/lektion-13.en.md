## 1. 🧍 Body Parts (Körperteile)

The body has many parts. Each part has a gender article (der / die / das). It is very important to memorize the article with the word!

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **der Kopf** | **deer kopf** | the head |
| **das Gesicht** | **dahs guh-ZEEKT** | the face |
| **das Auge** | **dahs OW-guh** | the eye |
| **die Nase** | **dee NAH-zuh** | the nose |
| **der Mund** | **deer moont** | the mouth |
| **das Ohr** | **dahs ohr** | the ear |
| **der Hals** | **deer hahls** | the neck / throat |
| **die Schulter** | **dee SHOOL-ter** | the shoulder |
| **der Arm** | **deer arm** | the arm |
| **die Hand** | **dee hahnt** | the hand |
| **der Finger** | **deer FING-er** | the finger |
| **der Bauch** | **deer bowkh** | the belly / stomach |
| **der Rücken** | **deer RÜK-en** | the back |
| **das Bein** | **dahs bine** | the leg |
| **das Knie** | **dahs knee** | the knee |
| **der Fuß** | **deer fooss** | the foot |
| **die Zähne** | **dee TSAY-nuh** | the teeth (plural) |

> 💡 **Tip:** Body parts that occur as pairs are often feminine (die) — die Hand, die Schulter. But there are exceptions!

## 2. 🏥 At the Doctor — Useful Phrases (Beim Arzt — Nützliche Sätze)

When you go to the doctor in German, you need to express pain. The most common sentence: **"X tut mir weh"** = "X hurts me."

### How to say "It hurts":

| German | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Der Kopf tut mir weh.** | **deer kopf toot meer veh** | My head hurts. |
| **Der Bauch tut mir weh.** | **deer bowkh toot meer veh** | My stomach hurts. |
| **Die Hand tut mir weh.** | **dee hahnt toot meer veh** | My hand hurts. |
| **Das Bein tut mir weh.** | **dahs bine toot meer veh** | My leg hurts. |
| **Die Zähne tun mir weh.** | **dee TSAY-nuh toon meer veh** | My teeth hurt. |

### Illnesses and Conditions

| German | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Ich habe Fieber.** | **ich HAH-buh FEE-ber** | I have a fever. |
| **Ich habe Husten.** | **ich HAH-buh HOO-sten** | I have a cough. |
| **Ich habe Schnupfen.** | **ich HAH-buh SHNOOP-fen** | I have a runny nose. |
| **Ich habe Kopfschmerzen.** | **ich HAH-buh KOPF-shmair-tsen** | I have a headache. |
| **Ich habe Halsschmerzen.** | **ich HAH-buh HAHLS-shmair-tsen** | I have a sore throat. |
| **Mir ist kalt.** | **meer ist kalt** | I feel cold. |
| **Mir ist heiß.** | **meer ist hyce** | I feel hot. |

> ⚠️ **Rule:** With **wehtun** we use the **Dative** case — **mir** (to me).  
> Der Kopf tut **mir** weh. = My head hurts. (Literally: The head hurts **me**.)

## 3. 📝 Grammar: haben + Schmerzen + der/mein

### 3.1 The verb "haben" (to have)

The verb **haben** changes with each pronoun. Memorize it well — it is one of the most used verbs after "sein."

| Pronoun | Conjugation | Pronunciation | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **ich** | **habe** | **HAH-buh** | Ich **habe** Fieber. |
| **du** | **hast** | **hahst** | Du **hast** Husten. |
| **Sie** (formal) | **haben** | **HAH-ben** | Sie **haben** Schnupfen. |
| **er / sie** | **hat** | **haht** | Er **hat** Kopfschmerzen. |
| **wir** | **haben** | **HAH-ben** | Wir **haben** Fieber. |
| **ihr** | **habt** | **hahbt** | Ihr **habt** Halsschmerzen. |
| **sie** (they) | **haben** | **HAH-ben** | Sie **haben** Schmerzen. |

### 3.2 mein / meine (Possessive with body parts)

In German, we say **"mein Kopf"** (my head) or **"meine Hand"** (my hand). The article determines the form:

| Original article | mein + article |
|-----------------|---------------|
| **der** (masculine) | **mein** — mein Kopf, mein Arm, mein Bauch |
| **die** (feminine) | **meine** — meine Hand, meine Nase, meine Schulter |
| **das** (neuter) | **mein** — mein Bein, mein Auge, mein Ohr |
| **die** (plural) | **meine** — meine Zähne, meine Augen, meine Ohren |

**Examples:**
- **Mein Kopf tut weh.** — My head hurts.
- **Meine Hand tut weh.** — My hand hurts.
- **Mein Bein tut weh.** — My leg hurts.
- **Meine Zähne tun weh.** — My teeth hurt.

> 🎯 **Notice:** With die (singular) and with plural we use **meine**. With der and das we use **mein**.

## 4. 💬 Conversation (Gespräch — Beim Arzt)

> **Patient:** Guten Tag, Herr Doktor!  
> *(Good day, Doctor!)*
> 
> **Arzt:** Guten Tag! Was fehlt Ihnen?  
> *(Good day! What's wrong with you?)*
> 
> **Patient:** Ich habe Kopfschmerzen und der Hals tut mir weh.  
> *(I have a headache and my throat hurts.)*
> 
> **Arzt:** Haben Sie auch Fieber?  
> *(Do you also have a fever?)*
> 
> **Patient:** Ja, ich habe Fieber. 39 Grad.  
> *(Yes, I have a fever. 39 degrees.)*
> 
> **Arzt:** Gut, ich untersuche Sie. Machen Sie bitte den Mund auf.  
> *(Good, I'll examine you. Please open your mouth.)*
> 
> **Patient:** Aaaaah...  
> *(Aaaaah...)*
> 
> **Arzt:** Ja, der Hals ist rot. Sie haben eine Erkältung. Ich gebe Ihnen ein Rezept.  
> *(Yes, the throat is red. You have a cold. I'll give you a prescription.)*
> 
> **Patient:** Vielen Dank, Herr Doktor!  
> *(Thank you very much, Doctor!)*
> 
> **Arzt:** Bitte schön! Gute Besserung!  
> *(You're welcome! Get well soon!)*

### Words from the conversation:

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **Was fehlt Ihnen?** | **vahs feelt EE-nen** | What's wrong with you? |
| **untersuchen** | **oon-ter-ZOO-khen** | to examine |
| **den Mund aufmachen** | **deen moont OWF-mah-khen** | to open the mouth |
| **rot** | **roht** | red |
| **die Erkältung** | **dee er-KEL-toong** | a cold (illness) |
| **das Rezept** | **dahs reh-TSEPT** | prescription |
| **Gute Besserung!** | **GOO-teh BES-er-oong** | Get well soon! |

## 6. 🧠 Quick Review (Schnelle Erinnerung)

- Body parts have articles: **der** (masculine), **die** (feminine), **das** (neuter)
- Pain: **X tut mir weh** (X hurts me)
- Illnesses: **Ich habe + illness name** (I have ...)
- Wishes: **Gute Besserung!** (Get well soon!)
- **mein** with der/das, **meine** with die/plural
