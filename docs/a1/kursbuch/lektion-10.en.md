## 1. 🚍 New Words (Neue Wörter)

First, let's learn the names of transport in German:

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **der Bus** | **deer booss** | the bus |
| **die Bahn** | **dee bahn** | the train / railway |
| **der Zug** | **deer tsook** | the train |
| **die S-Bahn** | **dee ess-bahn** | the suburban train |
| **die U-Bahn** | **dee oo-bahn** | the underground / metro |
| **der Bahnhof** | **deer BAHN-hof** | the train station |
| **die Haltestelle** | **dee HAL-tuh-shtel-uh** | the bus stop |
| **das Ticket** | **dahs TICK-et** | the ticket |
| **die Fahrkarte** | **dee FAR-kar-tuh** | the travel ticket |
| **der Fahrplan** | **deer FAR-plahn** | the timetable |

> 💡 **Question:** What's the difference between **Zug** and **Bahn**? Answer: **Zug** means a train (any train). **Bahn** means railway or train in general. People use them the same way most of the time.

## 2. 🚌 Means of Transport (Die Verkehrsmittel)

In Germany, there are many types of transport. Let's get to know them:

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **das Auto** | **dahs OW-toh** | the car |
| **das Fahrrad** | **dahs FAR-raht** | the bicycle |
| **das Motorrad** | **dahs mo-TOHR-raht** | the motorcycle |
| **das Taxi** | **dahs TAH-ksee** | the taxi |
| **der Lastwagen** | **deer LAST-vah-gen** | the truck |
| **das Schiff** | **dahs shif** | the ship |
| **das Flugzeug** | **dahs FLOOK-tsoyk** | the airplane |

### 🟢 The most important verb: **fahren**

The verb **fahren** means **to go** or **to travel** by a vehicle. It changes (irregular):

| Pronoun | Conjugation | Pronunciation |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| ich | **fahre** | **FAH-ruh** |
| du | **fährst** | **fayrst** |
| er / sie / es | **fährt** | **fayrt** |
| wir | **fahren** | **FAH-ren** |
| ihr | **fahrt** | **fahrt** |
| sie / Sie | **fahren** | **FAH-ren** |

### Examples:

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Ich fahre mit dem Bus.** | I go by bus. |
| **Du fährst mit der Bahn.** | You go by train. |
| **Er fährt mit dem Auto.** | He goes by car. |
| **Wir fahren mit der U-Bahn.** | We go by underground. |
| **Fahren Sie mit dem Zug?** | Do you go by train? |

> ⚠️ **Careful!** With means of transport we use **mit** (meaning "by").  
> **mit dem Bus** = by bus  
> **mit der Bahn** = by train  
> **mit dem Fahrrad** = by bicycle

## 3. 📝 How to buy a ticket? (Ein Ticket kaufen)

The most important sentences you need to know at a train station or bus stop:

| German | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Eine Fahrkarte bitte!** | **EYE-nuh FAR-kar-tuh BIT-uh** | A ticket, please! |
| **Einmal nach Berlin, bitte!** | **EYN-mahl nahkh ber-LEEN BIT-uh** | One to Berlin, please! |
| **Hin und zurück?** | **hin unt tsoo-RÜCK** | Round trip? |
| **Einfach, bitte.** | **EYN-fahkh BIT-uh** | One-way, please. |
| **Was kostet das?** | **vahs KOS-tet dahs** | How much does that cost? |
| **Das kostet 5 Euro.** | **dahs KOS-tet fünff OY-roh** | That costs 5 euros. |
| **Wo gibt es Fahrkarten?** | **voh gipt ess FAR-kar-ten** | Where can I get tickets? |

## 4. 💬 Conversation (Gespräch) — At the Train Station

This is a conversation between **Anna** (a tourist) and **Herr Meier** (a station employee).

> **Anna:** Guten Tag! Ich möchte eine Fahrkarte nach München.
> *(Good day! I'd like a ticket to Munich.)*

> **Herr Meier:** Guten Tag! Einfach oder hin und zurück?
> *(Good day! One-way or round trip?)*

> **Anna:** Hin und zurück, bitte.
> *(Round trip, please.)*

> **Herr Meier:** Das kostet 60 Euro.
> *(That costs 60 euros.)*

> **Anna:** OK. Hier ist das Geld. Wann fährt der Zug?
> *(OK. Here is the money. When does the train leave?)*

> **Herr Meier:** Der Zug fährt um 14 Uhr. Gleis 3.
> *(The train leaves at 2 PM. Platform 3.)*

> **Anna:** Und wie lange dauert die Fahrt?
> *(And how long does the trip take?)*

> **Herr Meier:** Die Fahrt dauert 2 Stunden.
> *(The trip takes 2 hours.)*

> **Anna:** Vielen Dank! Auf Wiedersehen!
> *(Thank you very much! Goodbye!)*

> **Herr Meier:** Bitte schön! Gute Reise!
> *(You're welcome! Have a good trip!)*

### Important questions from the conversation:

| German question | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| **Ich möchte eine Fahrkarte.** | **ikh MÖKH-tuh EYE-nuh FAR-kar-tuh** | I'd like a ticket. |
| **Einfach oder hin und zurück?** | **EYN-fahkh OH-der hin unt tsoo-RÜCK** | One-way or round trip? |
| **Wann fährt der Zug?** | **van fayrt deer tsook** | When does the train leave? |
| **Wie lange dauert die Fahrt?** | **vee LAHNG-uh DOW-ert dee fart** | How long does the trip take? |
| **Von welchem Gleis?** | **fon VEL-khem glice** | From which platform? |
| **Wo ist der Busbahnhof?** | **voh ist deer BOOSS-bahn-hof** | Where is the bus station? |
| **Gute Reise!** | **GOO-tuh RY-zuh** | Have a good trip! |

## 6. 🎫 The preposition **mit** — (by / with)

In German, when you talk about the means of transport you use, use **mit** + Dative.

| Vehicle | In German | Translation |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| bus | **mit dem Bus** | by bus |
| train | **mit dem Zug** | by train |
| underground | **mit der U-Bahn** | by underground |
| car | **mit dem Auto** | by car |
| bicycle | **mit dem Fahrrad** | by bicycle |
| airplane | **mit dem Flugzeug** | by airplane |
| taxi | **mit dem Taxi** | by taxi |
| ship | **mit dem Schiff** | by ship |

### ⚠️ Important note on Dative:

Bus, train, car... are all **der** (masculine), so they become **mit + dem**.
Notice:
- **der Bus** → **mit dem Bus** ✅
- **der Zug** → **mit dem Zug** ✅
- **der Bahnhof** → **am Bahnhof** ✅ (for a place)
- **die Bahn** (feminine) → **mit der Bahn** ✅
- **die S-Bahn** → **mit der S-Bahn** ✅
- **das Taxi** (neuter) → **mit dem Taxi** ✅

### Examples:

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Ich fahre mit dem Bus zur Schule.** | I go to school by bus. |
| **Meine Mutter fährt mit dem Auto zur Arbeit.** | My mother goes to work by car. |
| **Kommst du mit der U-Bahn?** | Do you come by underground? |
| **Wir reisen mit dem Flugzeug.** | We travel by airplane. |

> 🎯 **The rule simply:**
> - **fahren** = to go (by vehicle)
> - **mit** + vehicle = by (bus / train / car...)
> - **mit dem** for der-words and das-words
> - **mit der** for die-words
