# n-Declination (Weak Masculine Nouns)
---

## 🎯 Lesson goals

- Recognise weak masculine nouns that add **-n / -en** in every case except Nominative.
- Learn the full declension tables for both definite (**der**) and indefinite (**ein**) articles.
- Practice with common everyday nouns.

---

## 📚 Core rule

Weak masculine nouns (often called **n‑declension** nouns) end in **-(e)n** in the Accusative, Dative and Genitive. The Nominative stays unchanged. This pattern applies to both definite and indefinite articles.

---

### Declension table

| Case | Definite article (der) | Indefinite article (ein) |
|------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Nominative | der Student | ein Student |
| Accusative | den Studenten | einen Studenten |
| Dative | dem Studenten | einem Studenten |
| Genitive | des Studenten | eines Studenten |

---

### Common nouns with n‑declension

- der Student
- der Name
- der Herr
- der Mensch
- der Nachbar
- der Kollege
- der Junge
- der Franzose
- der Journalist
- der Polizist

---

### Examples

- **der Student** → **den Studenten**, **dem Studenten**, **des Studenten**
- **der Name** → **den Namen**, **dem Namen**, **des Namens**
- **der Herr** → **den Herrn**, **dem Herrn**, **des Herrn**
