## 🎯 Lesson Goals

- Understand the function of the Past Perfect in German (events that happened before another past event).
- Learn how to form it using the auxiliary **haben** or **sein** + Partizip II.
- Identify which verbs take **sein** as the auxiliary.
- Apply the form in everyday narratives.

---

## 📚 Core Rule

The Past Perfect (Plusquamperfekt) is built with:
1. The auxiliary verb **haben** or **sein** in the simple past (Präteritum).
2. The **Partizip II** (past participle) of the main verb.

| Verb (infinitive) | Auxiliary (Präteritum) |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **gehen** (to go) | **sein** |
| **kommen** (to come) | **sein** |
| **fahren** (to drive) | **sein** |
| **arbeiten** (to work) | **haben** |
| **sehen** (to see) | **haben** |

**Formation**: *[auxiliary in Präteritum] + [Partizip II]*

**Examples**:
- **Ich hatte das Buch gelesen.** — I had read the book.
- **Sie war nach Hause gegangen.** — She had gone home.
- **Wir hatten das Essen gekocht.** — We had cooked the meal.
- **Er war im Krankenhaus gewesen.** — He had been in the hospital.

---

## 📝 Example Sentences with Translation

| German sentence | English translation |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Ich **hatte** den Film **gesehen**. | I had seen the film. |
| Wir **waren** im Park **gelaufen**. | We had walked in the park. |
| Er **hatte** das Haus **gebaut**. | He had built the house. |
| Du **warst** beim Arzt **gewesen**. | You had been at the doctor. |
